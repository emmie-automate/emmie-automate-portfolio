The challenge

A Dubai real estate company had 20-30k leads, a mix of old backlog and new incoming, and no way to qualify them before they reached sales. Two problems hid in one project: bulk qualification that wouldn't get their WhatsApp number flagged by Meta, and instant handling of fresh leads. Most builds get this wrong by treating them the same.

The system

  • A Claude-powered agent ("Sarah") holds a natural WhatsApp conversation and extracts five qualification fields from freeform replies: budget, buy vs. rent, financing status, preferred area, and timeline.
  • Each answer feeds a weighted scoring engine. Leads are tagged Hot, Warm, or Cold automatically, using thresholds the client signed off on, not ones the AI guessed.
  • Hot leads create a deal in the pipeline with a structured summary note, so an agent knows everything in 10 seconds.
  • Cold leads drop into a nurture sequence with timed re-engagement.
  • Backlog handled with Meta-approved templates in throttled batches to protect number quality; new leads trigger instantly via webhook.

The challenge

Running a consistent YouTube Shorts channel normally takes 15 to 20 hours a week of research, scripting, editing, publishing, and analytics review. The target for this build: keep the channel publishing daily on under three hours of human time per week, without lowering editorial standards.

The system, in five stages

  • Ideation: five agriculture news feeds merged and analyzed; Claude proposes ranked video ideas in a "farm-business mentor" voice.
  • Scripting: every script must deliver both practical how-to and business ROI. That dual-angle rule is enforced in the prompt itself, and the AI is barred from inventing figures.
  • Production: scripts rendered into Shorts via JSON2Video with stock footage matched from Pexels.
  • Publishing: scheduled uploads via the YouTube Data API, metadata prepared automatically.
  • Learning loop: weekly analytics feed back into ideation, so the pipeline gets smarter about what performs.
  • Email approval gates sit between every stage. One tap from a phone approves the next step.

The challenge

A psychology content brand needed a steady stream of quote graphics for social feeds. Producing them manually meant writing a quote, briefing a designer, waiting for the visual, and posting it. That cycle collapsed whenever the team got busy, and consistency is the whole game in content.

The system

  • A schedule trigger starts each run and pulls the next title idea from a Google Sheets queue.
  • A Quote Generator agent writes the psychology fact and caption copy, then logs it to the sheet.
  • A second Design Generator agent turns that copy into a visual brief, generates the image, and renders the final graphic from a locked brand template so every post stays on-brand.
  • Finished assets are uploaded, and the sheet is updated with the image record, giving the team a full archive of what was produced and when.

How I Work

How a project runs, start to finish.

Every engagement follows the same four stages. The one decision I never automate is where human judgment belongs.

Stage 1

Map the bottleneck

We find where hours are actually leaking (lead response, intake, content, follow-up) and what fixing it is worth to the business.

Stage 2

Design the human gates

Before any node is built, we agree on where judgment stays human: scoring thresholds, approval points, escalation rules. You sign these off, not the AI.

Stage 3

Build & validate

I build in stages and test each one with live runs (a hot lead, a cold lead, empty data, a failed API call) before moving on. You see real executions, not mock-ups.

Stage 4

Hand over & support

You get a walkthrough recording, documentation, and error handling on every external call, so your team can run the system without me. Retainers are available if you want it to keep improving.
